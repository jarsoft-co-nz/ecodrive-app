# EcoDrive Safety Guidance

EcoDrive is an assistance tool. It does not replace driver judgement, road rules, road signs, official directions, vehicle instruments, or current conditions.

The driver remains responsible for safely and lawfully operating the vehicle at all times.

- Do not interact with EcoDrive while driving unless doing so is lawful and safe.
- Mount the device so it does not obstruct vision or controls.
- Ignore any route, prompt, estimate, or recommendation that is unsafe, unlawful, unclear, delayed, or inconsistent with current conditions.
- Never rely on EcoDrive as an emergency, collision-avoidance, autonomous-driving, mechanical-diagnostic, or safety-critical system.
- Stop using EcoDrive or connected hardware if it appears to interfere with vehicle operation.
- Install and use OBD-II adapters in accordance with vehicle and adapter manufacturer instructions.

Road, route, slope, traffic, speed, GPS, fuel, and vehicle information can be incomplete, delayed, inaccurate, or unavailable. Driving involves inherent risks that software cannot eliminate.

For emergencies, stop safely and contact the appropriate emergency or roadside service.
